Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about VA home loans and working with Veterans Own Mortgage.

You may qualify if you are an active duty service member, veteran, National Guard or Reserve member, or surviving spouse of a veteran. Eligibility is based on your length and type of service. Contact us and we will verify your eligibility at no cost.

Yes. Your VA loan benefit is reusable for life. As long as you have remaining entitlement or have paid off a previous VA loan, you can use it again to purchase another home or refinance.

The VA does not set a minimum credit score, but most lenders look for a score of 580 or higher. Veterans Own Mortgage works with veterans across a range of credit profiles and can help you understand your options even if your credit is less than perfect.

Most VA loans close in 30 to 45 days. With Veterans Own Mortgage, we work to keep the process as efficient as possible. Once you submit your application, we will give you a clear timeline based on your specific situation.

Yes. The VA offers two refinance options: the Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL) for lowering your rate on an existing VA loan, and the VA Cash Out refinance for accessing your home equity. Both can save you significant money.

As of 2020, there are no loan limits for veterans with full VA entitlement. If you have full entitlement you can borrow as much as a lender is willing to lend with no down payment required, regardless of the home price.
