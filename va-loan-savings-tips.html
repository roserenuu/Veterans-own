Maximizing Your Savings With A VA Home Loan

The VA loan benefit can reduce major barriers, but the best savings come from understanding the full loan structure before you sign.

Last reviewed June 20266 minute read

1. Compare More Than The Payment

A low payment can hide higher costs. Compare interest rate, APR, lender fees, discount points, credits, funding fee treatment, and total cash to close.

2. Use The No PMI Benefit Wisely

VA backed loans do not require monthly private mortgage insurance. That can be a meaningful advantage compared with many low down payment conventional loans.

3. Check Your Funding Fee Exemption

If you may be exempt from the VA funding fee, confirm that early. A funding fee exemption can change your total loan amount and overall cost.

4. Ask About Seller Credits

Seller credits may help reduce cash needed at closing, but they must fit VA and lender rules. Build the offer with your loan officer and real estate agent before you submit it.

5. Review Refinance Options Carefully

If you already have a VA loan, a VA IRRRL may help reduce payment or move from an adjustable rate to a more stable loan. The savings should make sense after fees and closing costs.

Best practice: Ask for written side by side options. A good comparison makes it easier to see what saves money now versus what saves money over time.
