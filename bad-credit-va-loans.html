Bad Credit And VA Loans: What You Need To Know

Lower credit does not automatically end the conversation. VA loans look at the full borrower profile, but lender guidelines still matter.

Last reviewed June 20266 minute read

The VA does not set one universal minimum credit score for every VA loan. Lenders review credit history and may set their own credit score requirements. That is why one lender may say no while another may still review the file.

What Lenders Usually Review

  • Recent mortgage or rent payment history
  • Debt to income ratio and residual income
  • Bankruptcies, foreclosures, collections, and late payments
  • Cash reserves and overall stability
  • Whether there are strong compensating factors

Can A 580 Score Work?

Some VA lenders may review files around a 580 credit score, but approval is never guaranteed by score alone. The rest of the file has to support the loan. A borrower with stable income, manageable debts, and clean recent housing history may be viewed differently than a borrower with the same score and recent severe late payments.

Practical tip: If another lender turned you down, ask what the exact issue was. Was it score, debt ratio, residual income, recent late payments, or a waiting period? The answer tells you what to fix first.

Steps That May Help Before Applying

  1. Check your credit reports for errors.
  2. Bring past due accounts current if possible.
  3. Avoid opening new debt before applying.
  4. Keep documentation for any major credit event.
  5. Talk with a VA loan specialist before assuming you cannot qualify.
