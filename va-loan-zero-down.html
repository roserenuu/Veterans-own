Buying A Home

VA Loan Down Payments: The Truth About Zero Down

VA loans can be one of the strongest low cash to close options for eligible borrowers, but zero down still deserves a clear look at the full numbers.

Last reviewed June 20265 minute read

For eligible borrowers, a VA backed purchase loan may allow 100% financing when the sales price does not exceed the appraised value and the borrower qualifies. That is a major benefit compared with many conventional and FHA options.

Zero Down Is Not The Same As Zero Cost

A no down payment VA loan can still involve closing costs, prepaid taxes and insurance, inspections, appraisal costs, earnest money, and a VA funding fee if you are not exempt. Some costs may be paid by the seller, lender credits, or other allowed sources, but the structure has to be reviewed carefully.

Why Some Borrowers Still Put Money Down

Even when no down payment is required, a borrower may choose to put money down to lower the loan amount, reduce the VA funding fee tier in some cases, strengthen the offer, or create a lower monthly payment.

Smart move: Ask for a side by side estimate showing zero down, 5% down, and 10% down if you have cash available. The best choice depends on your payment goal and cash reserves.

What To Watch For

  • The home still needs to appraise and meet VA property requirements.
  • The lender still reviews income, credit, debts, assets, and residual income.
  • Seller credits can help, but they must follow VA and lender rules.
  • No monthly PMI is a major VA benefit, but the upfront funding fee may still apply.
